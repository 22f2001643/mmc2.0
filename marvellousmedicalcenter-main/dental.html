TOOTH COLOURED RESTORATIONS

Tooth-colored restorations, also known as composite or resin fillings, are dental treatments used to repair decayed or damaged teeth. They blend seamlessly with natural teeth, providing both aesthetic appeal and functional strength.

TOOTH SPACE CLOSURES

Tooth space closures involve cosmetic dental procedures that eliminate gaps between teeth, typically using bonding, veneers, or orthodontics. These treatments improve both the appearance and alignment of teeth, enhancing overall dental aesthetics.

TEETH WHITENING/ BLEACHING

Teeth whitening, also known as bleaching, is a cosmetic dental procedure that lightens the color of teeth, removing stains and discoloration. It enhances the brightness of teeth, improving the overall appearance of a person’s smile.

SCALING, TEETH CLEANING AND POLISHING

Scaling, teeth cleaning, and polishing are dental procedures that remove plaque, tartar, and surface stains from teeth. Scaling cleans below the gumline, while polishing smooths the tooth surfaces, promoting oral hygiene and leaving teeth feeling smooth and shiny.

PEDIATRIC DENTISTRY

Pediatric dentistry focuses on the oral health of children, from infancy through adolescence. It includes preventive care, education on proper oral hygiene, and treatment of dental issues in children to ensure healthy development of teeth and gums.

CROWNS, BRIDGES AND VENEERS

Crowns, bridges, and veneers are restorative and cosmetic dental treatments. Crowns cover damaged teeth to restore their shape and strength, bridges fill gaps left by missing teeth, and veneers are thin, custom-made shells applied to the front of teeth to improve appearance, covering imperfections such as discoloration or chips.

DENTURES – PARTIAL, COMPLETE AND IMMEDIATE DENTURES

Dentures are removable dental appliances used to replace missing teeth. Partial dentures fill in gaps for those with some remaining natural teeth, complete dentures replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw, and immediate dentures are placed right after tooth extraction to provide immediate replacement while the gums heal.

NORMAL AND SURGICAL EXTRACTIONS OF TOOTH

Normal extractions involve the simple removal of a tooth that is fully visible and easily accessible. Surgical extractions, on the other hand, are more complex procedures used for teeth that are impacted, broken, or not fully erupted, requiring incisions and sometimes bone removal for extraction.

ROOT CANAL TREATMENT, PULPOTOMY

Root canal treatment involves removing infected or damaged pulp from a tooth, followed by cleaning, disinfecting, and sealing the space to prevent further infection. Pulpotomy is a similar procedure for primary (baby) teeth, where only the infected pulp is removed while preserving the healthy portion. Both treatments aim to save the tooth and relieve pain.

DENTAL X-RAY

Dental X-rays are diagnostic tools that allow dentists to capture detailed images of teeth, bones, and surrounding tissues. They help identify hidden issues such as cavities, infections, bone loss, or impacted teeth that are not visible during a regular dental exam.

PREVENTIVE DENTISTRY

Preventive dentistry focuses on maintaining oral health and preventing dental problems before they occur. It includes regular check-ups, cleanings, fluoride treatments, sealants, and education on proper oral hygiene practices to avoid cavities, gum disease, and other dental issues.

FREE DENTAL CHECK-UP

A free dental check-up offers patients a thorough examination of their oral health at no cost. Dentists assess teeth and gums to identify potential issues early and provide recommendations for preventive care or necessary treatments.
