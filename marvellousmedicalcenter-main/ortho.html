BRACES

Braces are dental devices used to correct misaligned teeth and jaws, helping to improve both function and aesthetics. They consist of brackets, wires, and bands that gradually move teeth into the desired position, often requiring regular adjustments by an orthodontist.

CLEAR ALIGNERS

Clear aligners are a popular alternative to traditional braces, designed to gradually straighten teeth without the use of metal brackets and wires. These custom-made, transparent trays are removable and typically require fewer visits to the orthodontist, making them a convenient option for many patients.

RETAINER

Retainers are custom-made devices used to maintain the position of teeth after orthodontic treatment. They help prevent teeth from shifting back to their original positions and are essential for long-term treatment success, often worn full-time initially and then as needed.

ORTHODOTIC EXPANDERS

Orthodontic expanders are devices used to widen the upper jaw to create more space for teeth. They are particularly useful in young patients whose jaws are still developing and can help improve alignment and overall dental health.

SPACE MAINTAINERS

Space maintainers are dental devices used to hold the space for teeth that may be lost prematurely due to decay or injury. They prevent adjacent teeth from shifting into the empty space, ensuring proper alignment when the permanent teeth erupt.

ORTHODONTIC HEADGEAR

Orthodontic headgear is an external device that helps correct bite and jaw alignment issues by applying gentle pressure. It is often used in conjunction with braces and is essential for guiding the growth of the jaw in younger patients.

JAW SURGERY

Orthognathic or jaw surgery is sometimes required to correct severe bite issues or jaw misalignments that cannot be resolved with braces alone. This surgical intervention aims to improve both functionality and facial aesthetics, often involving a multidisciplinary approach with orthodontists and oral surgeons.

CERAMIC BRACES

Ceramic braces are similar to traditional metal braces but use clear or tooth-colored brackets to blend in with the teeth. They offer a more aesthetically pleasing option for patients concerned about the visibility of their orthodontic treatment.

LINGUAL BRACES

Lingual braces are attached to the back of the teeth, making them virtually invisible from the front. They provide an effective treatment option for patients looking for discreet orthodontic solutions while still benefiting from the alignment capabilities of traditional braces.
